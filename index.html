Demographics

Explore actual and forecasted demographic data from the 2021 Census and SGS Economics on the City of Melbourne Council. Emphasis is placed on population, rent, income and car ownership.

The City of Melbourne Council is composed of the following suburbs:

  • Carlton
  • Docklands
  • East Melbourne
  • Kensington
  • Melbourne
  • North Melbourne
  • Parkville
  • Port Melbourne
  • South Yarra
  • Southbank
  • West Melbourne
  • South Wharf

South Wharf is excluded from the following data visualisations due to low population numbers.

Forecasted population

SGS Economics produced forecasts for the City of Melbourne for each year from 2020 to 2040. This included population forecasts broken down by age group and gender.

The suburb of Melbourne has the highest population for all time periods, followed by Southbank. This could be due to these suburbs' higher density residential buildings, relative to the other suburbs in the City of Melbourne.

For more information on SGS Economics' forecasts, visit City of Melbourne Open Data.

Median characteristics

Here is a visual representation of selected characteristics of each suburb of the City of Melbourne.

Median age

East Melbourne and Port Melbourne have the highest median age of 42 years of age.

Parkville and Carlton have the lowest median ages of 26 and 27, respectively.

Median weekly rent ($)

Port Melbourne has the highest median rent of $540 per week.

Carlton has the lowest median rent of $365 per week.

Median personal weekly income ($)

East Melbourne has the highest median personal income of $1,532 per week.

Parkville has the lowest median personal income of $586 per week.

Car ownership

Melbourne had the highest share of no vehicles and one vehicle per dwelling at 61% and 33%, respectively. Port Melbourne had the lowest share of no vehicles per dwelling at 12%, but had the highest share of 2+ vehicles per dwelling. These findings can be attributed to the higher density residential buildings in Melbourne in comparison to Port Melbourne. As seen above, Port Melbourne has a higher median age, weekly rent and personal income than Melbourne, which could influence a person's willingness to use their discretionary income for purchasing vehicles.

Population breakdown

City of Melbourne

The vast majority of City of Melbourne residents are in the 25-34 year old age group, with the 20-24 and 35-44 year old age groups being the next two most populous age groups.

Let's take a look at the population breakdowns for Port Melbourne and Parkville, the suburbs with the highest and lowest median ages, respectively.

Parkville

Parkville's population heavily skews towards the 15-34 year old age bracket. This is reflected by its median age of 26.

The relatively low share of people in the other age brackets indicate that there may not be many families living in Parkville.

Port Melbourne

In contrast to Parkville, Port Melbourne has less people in the 15-24 year old age bracket, relative to other age brackets. This is reflected by its median age of 42.

This indicates that there may be more families living in Port Melbourne than in Parkville.
